## Project-Western-Frontier <img width="49.8" height="49.8" alt="LaughingRipWheelerGIF" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/5d98d4a9-08fc-42c2-bed2-4711b395cc22" />


🤠🚂 Western Frontier is a project that’s incredibly close to my heart, and one day I would love to share it with players all around the world! Inspired by games like Papers, Please and Quarantine Zone, the game puts you in the boots of an officer stationed at the gates of a brand-new frontier town. Your job is to inspect the passengers arriving by train and decide who deserves entry… and who should be turned away. 🛂🌵

But those choices won’t just affect a single moment, they will shape the future of your entire town. 🏘️⚖️ I also plan to introduce city-building and management mechanics, meaning every person you allow into the frontier could impact the economy, crime, resources, population growth, and the overall fate of your settlement. Every decision matters, creating a world that reacts directly to the player’s judgment and morality.

Although I haven’t had the chance to fully focus on the project yet due to working on my Space Adventures title 🚀✨, I’ve already begun building some exciting systems for Western Frontier. So far, I’ve created a random character generator that produces the passengers arriving from the train, each with unique traits and backgrounds. I’ve also designed the system to be highly modular and efficient, making it easy to expand with new categories and passenger types as development continues. 🧠⚙️

One of the biggest goals for Western Frontier is to make it a fully 3D first-person experience 🎮🔥. I’ve always loved creating games in 3D because it introduces new coding and design challenges that make development more rewarding and creative. More importantly, I believe a first-person 3D world will make the experience far more immersive, allowing players to truly feel like they are standing at the frontier gates, making difficult choices face-to-face as trains roll into town under the scorching western sun. 🌄🚂
